From B to C — What actually moves the needle

The funnel doesn't improve by accident. Here's what drives each rate — and what you can do about it.

Lever 1 — Offer Rate
More requests becoming offers
The offer rate tells you how many inbound requests your TAs actually engage with properly. A low rate means requests are being dropped, ignored, or unqualified without reaching offer stage.
🎯
Lead quality from marketing
Better-qualified traffic converts more easily. Are your marketing channels sending the right customers?
⚡
Speed of first response
Requests that get a response within 2 hours have significantly higher offer rates. Time kills deals.
🗂️
Pipeline hygiene & task discipline
TAs with overdue tasks let requests fall through the cracks. This is visible in your Salesforce Operations dashboard right now.
Lever 2 — Hit Rate
More offers converting to bookings
Once an offer is sent, does it convert? Hit rate is your TA's ability to create compelling offers and follow them through to confirmation. This is where expertise lives.
✍️
Offer quality & personalisation
Generic offers get ignored. Personalised, well-structured journeys get booked. Know your customer before you write.
📞
Follow-up discipline
Most bookings don't happen at first contact. Consistent, timely follow-up is the single biggest differentiator in hit rate.
🧠
Product knowledge & trust
A TA who knows the product deeply can handle objections, suggest alternatives, and build confidence. Training pays off here.
